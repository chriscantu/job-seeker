# Voice Audit Checklist

After drafting any content, audit against the candidate's voice guide at `references/voice-guide.md`.

## Rules to Check

| # | Rule | What to Look For |
|---|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Teach, don't perform** | Frame accomplishments as "here's what we did and what we learned," not "here's how impressive I am." |
| 2 | **Name problems plainly** | No corporate language dressing up challenges. If something is broken, say it's broken. |
| 3 | **"We" over "I"** | Default to "we" for team accomplishments. "I" only for personal decisions and reflections. Count uses of "I" — if more than 1-2 in the whole piece, rewrite. |
| 4 | **Concrete before abstract** | Lead with the number or the specific, then add meaning. Data before thesis. |
| 5 | **Dry, understated humor** | At least one wry aside, playful label, or self-deprecating moment. Not forced — if it doesn't fit, skip it. |
| 6 | **Generous with the how** | Share the actual process, methods, tools. Don't gatekeep. |
| 7 | **No buzzwords** | No "synergy," "leverage," "thought leadership," "uniquely positioned," "passionate." Write plainly. |

## Anti-Patterns to Flag

The draft should NOT sound like:
- **Career coach:** "I'm passionate about driving organizational excellence"
- **LinkedIn influencer:** "Here's what most leaders get wrong about X"
- **Corporate:** "I'm uniquely positioned to leverage my experience"
- **Self-congratulatory:** "I know what it takes to build X at scale"
- **Performative humility:** "I've been fortunate enough to lead..."

## Sentence-Level Patterns

- Medium-length sentences. Not clipped, not meandering.
- Colons and em dashes for emphasis, not exclamation points.
- Open paragraphs with context, not with "I."
- Close sections by pointing forward (what's next, what we're still learning).

After drafting, present a table:
