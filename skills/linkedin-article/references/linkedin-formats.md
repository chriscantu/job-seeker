# LinkedIn Content Formats

## Post (Regular)

- **Character limit:** 3,000 characters
- **Visible before "see more":** ~210 characters (first 2-3 lines)
- **Algorithm reach:** High — shows in followers' feeds
- **Best for:** Quick takes, data-driven hooks, driving traffic to articles
- **Best posting time:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am in author's timezone
- **Link handling:** LinkedIn penalizes links *in* posts. Place links in the first comment instead.
- **Images:** Single image posts outperform text-only. Carousels (PDF uploads) get highest engagement.

### Post Structure

1. **Hook (lines 1-2):** Must stand alone before "see more" fold. Lead with a surprising stat, contrarian take, or concrete claim.
2. **Body (lines 3-15):** Build the argument. Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). Use line breaks for scannability.
3. **Close (last 2-3 lines):** Call to action, forward-looking statement, or memorable one-liner.
4. **First comment:** Post the article link here immediately after publishing.

## Article (LinkedIn Newsletter/Long-form)

- **Character limit:** None
- **Algorithm reach:** Lower than posts — does not appear natively in feed
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, SEO, permanent profile content, Featured section
- **Supports:** Inline images, headers, links, bold/italic, blockquotes
- **Ideal length:** 800-1,500 words (~4-7 minute read)
- **Images:** Include 2-4 images. Header image is required. Inline images break up text and increase time-on-page.

### Article Structure

1. **Title:** Clear, specific, searchable. Under 70 characters.
2. **Subtitle:** One-line thesis statement.
3. **Sections:** 4-6 sections with H2 headers. Each section should be scannable independently.
4. **Sources:** Link inline at point of reference AND collect in a sources section at the bottom.
5. **Images:** Place after the paragraph that introduces the concept, with caption/attribution below.

## Teaser Post + Article Combo

The highest-impact approach: publish the article first, then post a teaser that hooks with the thesis and drives clicks. The teaser should make the argument in miniature — not just say "I wrote an article."

### Teaser Formula

```
[Surprising stat or contrarian claim — 1-2 lines]

[Brief expansion — why this matters — 3-5 lines]

[What the article covers — 1-2 lines]

Link in comments.
```
