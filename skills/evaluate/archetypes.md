# Evaluate — VPE Archetypes

Six archetypes for VP / Senior Director of Engineering roles. For each:
- **Key JD signals** — keywords and phrases that indicate this archetype
- **Lead proof points** — which resume lines to emphasize in Block B and Block E
- **Framing strategy** — how to position the candidate's background for this context

---

## 1. Platform-Scale

**Key JD signals:** "scale the engineering org", "grow the team", "Series B/C/D",
"build infrastructure for growth", "reliability at scale", "platform organization"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Led 60 engineers across 8 international teams"
- "Led $50M+ frontend modernization across 125+ repositories and 65+ global teams"
- "Led 25+ engineers across 5 international platform teams supporting $2.4B in booking revenue"
- "Scaled teams to multinational organizations across US, Europe, Africa, and Asia"

**Framing strategy:** Lead with team size and organizational scope. Emphasize the
multinational complexity — most candidates at this level haven't managed 60 engineers
across 8 time zones. Connect delivery transformation work to the scaling context:
"scale doesn't work without reliable delivery."

---

## 2. Early-Stage Builder

**Key JD signals:** "first VP of Engineering", "build the engineering function",
"founding team", "0 to 1", "seed/Series A", "define engineering culture",
"hire the first engineers"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Director of Front End Platforms | Babylon Health" — joined during rapid international expansion, built platform capability across US and Europe
- "Pioneered weekly mobile release cadence" — established new practices from scratch
- "Architected micro-frontend infrastructure" — foundational architecture work
- "Established architectural patterns and design standards adopted across Expedia's enterprise engineering organization"

**Framing strategy:** Acknowledge this is not the primary archetype, but bridge
through platform-building experience. Lead with the Babylon Health context
(joined during scale, built the platform function) and the "established from scratch"
proof points. Be honest about company stage experience: Babylon was growth-stage,
Vrbo was enterprise. Don't overclaim 0→1 startup experience.

---

## 3. Transformation

**Key JD signals:** "modernize the engineering org", "technical transformation",
"improve delivery", "reduce tech debt", "engineering effectiveness", "change
management", "enable product velocity", "broken delivery"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Transformed CI/CD from 1-5% to 95-99% reliability" — the anchor transformation story
- "Reduced localization deployment from 6 months to minutes, unlocking $12M+ revenue"
- "Achieved 85% design system adoption across 185 repositories in 6 months via AI-driven automation"
- "Delivered $18M+ in business value through delivery transformation"
- "Transformed CI/CD from 1% to 95% success rate" (Babylon)
- "Achieved 20x increase in release velocity" (Vrbo)

**Framing strategy:** This is the strongest archetype — lead aggressively with
the CI/CD transformation numbers. They are specific (1–5% → 95–99%), business-
connected ($18M+), and repeatable (demonstrated at Babylon AND Procore). Frame
as a specialization, not a coincidence: "This is the work I seek out."

---

## 4. Turnaround

**Key JD signals:** "turn around", "struggling team", "rebuild trust",
"engineering credibility", "previous leadership didn't work out",
"team morale", "delivery has stalled", "technical debt has accumulated"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Transformed CI/CD from 1% to 95% success rate" (Babylon) — inherited a broken system
- "Transformed CI/CD from 1-5% to 95-99% reliability" (Procore) — same pattern, larger scale
- "Led migration from monolithic to microservices architecture" (Vrbo)
- "Reduced localization deployment from 6 months to minutes"

**Framing strategy:** Similar to Transformation but emphasize inheriting broken
situations rather than greenfield transformation. Lead with "I've done this twice at
different scales." Add the human/team dimension: transformation only works if you
bring the team with you. Reference ShapeUp adoption at Vrbo as evidence of culture
reset, not just technical fix.

## 5. DX / Internal Platform

**Key JD signals:** "developer experience", "internal platform", "platform engineering",
"design systems", "CI/CD", "developer productivity", "tooling", "frontend platform",
"monorepo", "developer tooling", "DX team", "platform team"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Director of Front End Platforms | Babylon Health" — exact title match
- "Led 30 engineers across 5 platform teams (US, Europe)"
- "85% design system adoption across 185 repositories in 6 months via AI-driven automation"
- "Architected micro-frontend infrastructure deployable as single artifact, enabling 3x faster feature delivery"
- "Pioneered weekly mobile release cadence through automated testing, feature flags, and progressive rollouts"
- "CI/CD Optimization, Quality Automation, Feature Flags, Progressive Rollouts" (Skills section)

**Framing strategy:** This is the primary archetype — the most direct match to the
candidate's background. Lead with the "Director of Front End Platforms" title and the
5 platform teams at Babylon. Emphasize breadth: CI/CD + design systems + micro-frontend
+ mobile release cadence. The 185-repo design system adoption story is particularly
strong because it shows platform work that actually gets adopted.

## 6. AI-Integrated Engineering

**Key JD signals:** "AI adoption", "AI-powered workflows", "GitHub Copilot",
"AI tooling", "LLM integration", "developer AI", "AI-assisted engineering",
"embed AI across the org", "AI productivity"

**Lead proof points from resume-ats.md:**
- "Achieved 85% design system adoption across 185 repositories in 6 months via AI-driven automation"
- "Created contextual, customizable platform enabling product re-envisioning and supporting AI-powered product initiatives"
- "Partnered with VP of Product, VP of Engineering, and VP of Customer Success on technology strategy"

**Framing strategy:** This is an emerging archetype for Chris — be honest that the
experience is recent but directly relevant. Lead with the AI-driven automation for
design system adoption as the concrete proof point. Position as "I've started
integrating AI into platform engineering work and I'm actively building this
capability." Complement with the platform foundation: AI adoption at scale requires
strong platform infrastructure, which is the primary expertise.

---

## Hybrid Roles

If a role fits two archetypes, name both and identify the primary. Common combinations:

- **Transformation + Platform-Scale** — modernize while scaling (common at Series C+)
- **DX/Platform + Transformation** — fix platform delivery while building the team
- **Early-Stage + DX/Platform** — first platform VP at a startup

For hybrid roles, lead with the primary archetype's proof points, then bridge to the
secondary. Do not split effort equally — pick the archetype the hiring manager most
needs and lead there.
